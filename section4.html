Global Conflict Visualization

This section focuses on geographic perspectives of war: where wars occur, how long they tend to last in different locations, and how intense conflict has been across broad world regions. We combine a choropleth map, a bubble map, a regional dumbbell chart and the bivariate choropleth to summarize war frequency and duration in a spatially-aware way.

Choropleth Map — Number of Wars per Country

Choropleth placeholder

Countries are shaded according to how many wars in the dataset are associated with their territory. Darker shades indicate locations with a higher frequency of recorded wars over the 1816–2001 period.

Bubble Map — Average War Duration per Country

Bubble map placeholder

On top of a neutral world map, each country that appears in the dataset is marked with a circle whose size reflects the average duration of wars fought there. This highlights countries where conflicts tend to be unusually short or long, in a geographically intuitive way.

Regional Dumbbell Chart — War Frequency vs Average Duration

Dumbbell chart placeholder

For the regions with the most recorded wars, this dumbbell chart compares two normalized metrics side by side: how frequently wars occur and how long they last on average. Each horizontal line represents a geographic region, with endpoints showing its relative war frequency and relative average duration on a common scale.

Bivariate Choropleth Map — War Frequency × Average Duration

Bivariate choropleth placeholder

This map encodes two dimensions of conflict intensity at the same time: how frequently wars occur in each country (war count) and how long they last on average (duration). Countries are grouped into categories such as “High frequency / High duration” or “Low frequency / Low duration”, allowing us to quickly spot places where conflict is both frequent and protracted.

Section 1

Short summary: This project analyzes global war patterns by geography and purpose, building visual narratives from data.

WARS BY LOCATION AND PURPOSE (1816–2001)

Introduction

Identifies the location and purpose of all 464 major wars since 1816, expanding substantially on previous efforts by including wars fought in all polities, including pre-independent kingdoms and empires. The data complements standard war datasets that characterize wars only by the state participants that fight them. Compatible with the From Empire to Nation-state dataset.

Research Questions

  1. How many wars occurred in each region, and how do purposes vary across them?
  2. How has the number of wars changed over time for each type or purpose?
  3. Which types of wars tend to last longer on average?

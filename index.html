WARS BY LOCATION AND PURPOSE (1816–2001)

Introduction

Identifies the location and purpose of all 464 major wars since 1816, expanding substantially on previous efforts by including wars fought in all polities, including pre-independent kingdoms and empires. The data complements standard war datasets that characterize wars only by the state participants that fight them. Compatible with the From Empire to Nation-state dataset.

Research Questions

  1. Which ten regions have experienced the highest number of wars?
  2. How did the number of wars change before and after 1914 in each region?
  3. What temporal patterns emerge in the distribution of wars by region and decade?
  4. How does the composition of war purposes vary across regions?
  5. What proportion of wars were ethnic compared to non-ethnic?
  6. Within each category (Ethnic / Non-ethnic), which three regions experienced the most wars?

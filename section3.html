About This Section

This section focuses on the temporal evolution of warfare. Instead of looking at durations or spatial patterns, we ask a simple question: how often do wars start in each decade between 1816 and 2000 by purpose?

Line Chart — Wars Started per Decade, by Purpose (1816–2000)

Timeline chart placeholder

Each colored line represents a different war purpose. By tracking these lines across decades, we can see not only how frequently wars occurred, but also how the dominant motives behind them shifted over time — from territorial expansion in the 19th century to more ideological and intra-state conflicts in the 20th century.

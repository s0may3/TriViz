About This Section

This section explores how long wars typically last. We start with an overall histogram, then compare distributions by war purpose, and finally summarize spread and central tendency with boxplots. Most wars in the dataset are short and end within a few years, regardless of their purpose. Only a small number of wars—especially ethnic and secessionist ones—last much longer, creating a long-tailed distribution of conflict duration.
